Introduction

DiscordNomic is a Discord server used for playing Nomic: a game about law, self-modification and paradox.

This webpage serves as records for the game, including a logical organization for its rules, its players and information about the current and past games.

Immutable rules

311. Supremacy of the rules
All players must always abide by all the rules then in effect, in the form in which they are then in effect. The rules in the Initial Set are in effect whenever a game begins. The Initial Set consists of Rules 101-116 (immutable) and 201-213 (mutable).

All rule changes enacted in previous games and interim periods are also in effect.

This rule takes precedence over, and nullifies, 101.

Initial set, Rule 101,
Amended in the interlude between games 1 and 2,
Amended by Proposal 311 (transmuted by Proposal 312)

102. Definition of immutable and mutable
Initially rules in the 100's are immutable and rules in the 200's are mutable. Rules subsequently enacted or transmuted (that is, changed from immutable to mutable or vice versa) may be immutable or mutable regardless of their numbers, and rules in the Initial Set may be transmuted regardless of their numbers.

Initial set, Rule 102

103. Definition of rule-change
A rule-change is any of the following:

  1. the enactment, repeal, or amendment of a mutable rule;
  2. the enactment, repeal, or amendment of an amendment of a mutable rule; or
  3. the transmutation of an immutable rule into a mutable rule or vice versa.

(Note: This definition implies that, at least initially, all new rules are mutable; immutable rules, as long as they are immutable, may not be amended or repealed; mutable rules, as long as they are mutable, may be amended or repealed; any rule of any status may be transmuted; no rule is absolutely immune to change.)

Initial set, Rule 103

104. Voting and adoption of rule-changes
All rule-changes proposed in the proper way shall be voted on. They will be adopted if and only if they receive the required number of votes.

Initial set, Rule 104

105. Universal and mandatory suffrage
Every player is an eligible voter. Every eligible voter must participate in every vote on rule-changes.

Initial set, Rule 105

106. Writing of rule-changes
All proposed rule-changes shall be written down before they are voted on. If they are adopted, they shall guide play in the form in which they were voted on.

Initial set, Rule 106

107. Applying of rule-changes
No rule-change may take effect earlier than the moment of the completion of the vote that adopted it, even if its wording explicitly states otherwise. No rule-change may have retroactive application.

Initial set, Rule 107

108. Numbering of rule-changes
Each proposed rule-change shall be given a number for reference. The numbers shall begin with 301, and each rule-change proposed in the proper way shall receive the next successive integer, whether or not the proposal is adopted.

If a rule is repealed and reenacted, it receives the number of the proposal to reenact it. If a rule is amended or transmuted, it receives the number of the proposal to amend or transmute it. If an amendment is amended or repealed, the entire rule of which it is a part receives the number of the proposal to amend or repeal the amendment.

Initial set, Rule 108

109. Transmutation of immutable rules into mutable rules
Rule-changes that transmute immutable rules into mutable rules may be adopted if and only if the vote is unanimous among the eligible voters. Transmutation shall not be implied, but must be stated explicitly in a proposal to take effect.

Initial set, Rule 109

110. Precedence of immutable rules
In a conflict between a mutable and an immutable rule, the immutable rule takes precedence and the mutable rule shall be entirely void. For the purposes of this rule a proposal to transmute an immutable rule does not "conflict" with that immutable rule.

Initial set, Rule 110

111. Debating of rule-changes
If a rule-change as proposed is unclear, ambiguous, paradoxical, or destructive of play, or if it arguably consists of two or more rule-changes compounded or is an amendment that makes no difference, or if it is otherwise of questionable value, then the other players may suggest amendments or argue against the proposal before the vote. A reasonable time must be allowed for this debate. The proponent decides the final form in which the proposal is to be voted on and, unless the Judge has been asked to do so, also decides the time to end debate and vote.

Initial set, Rule 111

112. Winning of the game
The state of affairs that constitutes winning may not be altered from achieving n points to any other state of affairs. The magnitude of n and the means of earning points may be changed, and rules that establish a winner when play cannot continue may be enacted and (while they are mutable) be amended or repealed.

Initial set, Rule 112

113. Forfeiting
A player always has the option to forfeit the game rather than continue to play or incur a game penalty. No penalty worse than losing, in the judgment of the player to incur it, may be imposed.

Initial set, Rule 113

114. Minimum of mutable rules
There must always be at least one mutable rule. The adoption of rule-changes must never become completely impermissible.

Initial set, Rule 114

115. Scope of rule-changes
Rule-changes that affect rules needed to allow or apply rule-changes are as permissible as other rule-changes. Even rule-changes that amend or repeal their own authority are permissible. No rule-change or type of move is impermissible solely on account of the self-reference or self-application of a rule.

Initial set, Rule 115

116. Scope of the rules
Whatever is not prohibited or regulated by a rule is permitted and unregulated, with the sole exception of changing the rules, which is permitted only when a rule or set of rules explicitly or implicitly permits it.

Initial set, Rule 116

Mutable rules

Players

301. Playing order
Players shall alternate according to the list maintained in the Discord channel #playing-order, taking one whole turn apiece. Players who join after the start of the game shall be added onto the end of this list, which may otherwise only be updated by vote. Turns may not be skipped or passed, and parts of turns may not be omitted. All players begin with zero points.

Initial set, Rule 201,
Amended, Proposal 301D

Turns & game time

202. Parts of a turn
One turn consists of two parts in this order:

  1. proposing one rule-change and having it voted on, and
  2. throwing one die once and adding the number of points on its face to one's score.

In mail and computer games, instead of throwing a die, players subtract 291 from the ordinal number of their proposal and multiply the result by the fraction of favorable votes it received, rounded to the nearest integer. (This yields a number between 0 and 10 for the first player, with the upper limit increasing by one each turn; more points are awarded for more popular proposals.)

Initial set, Rule 202

320. Regular leave from Nomic
Nomic is closed every week for a time period from Saturday 01:00 to Sunday 09:00 (reckoned in UTC). No action taken during this time is considered part of the Nomic game. Outside of this time, game actions are as valid as in the rest of the rule set.

Rules

211. Ordinal precedence
If two or more mutable rules conflict with one another, or if two or more immutable rules conflict with one another, then the rule with the lowest ordinal number takes precedence.

If at least one of the rules in conflict explicitly says of itself that it defers to another rule (or type of rule) or takes precedence over another rule (or type of rule), then such provisions shall supersede the numerical method for determining precedence.

If two or more rules claim to take precedence over one another or to defer to one another, then the numerical method again governs.

Initial set, Rule 211

Voting

207. Equal representation
Each player always has exactly one vote.

After a vote

302. Rule change adoption
A rule-change is adopted if and only if the vote meets at least a simple majority of all eligible players. If a rule-change acquires unanimous support, then a quantity of bonus points equal to half the assenting votes (rounded down) is awarded to the player.

Initial set, Rule 203,
Amended, Proposal 302

205. Effectiveness
An adopted rule-change takes full effect at the moment of the completion of the vote that adopted it.

Initial set, Rule 205

206. Unsuccessful turn penalty
When a proposed rule-change is defeated, the player who proposed it loses 10 points.

Initial set, Rule 206

304. No opposition reward
Rule 204 is repealed. Players shall not receive any points for having voted against a rule that passed.

Enacted, Proposal 304

Proposal 319
In calculating the score for successful legislation, take the difference between the highest scoring player and the player who just passed legislation. Then divide by the number of players, and reward that quotient, rounded down to the nearest integer, as additional points.

For example, if the player with the highest score has 70 points, and I have 0, this rule would grant me (70 - 0) / 7 = 10 additional points. Further, this rule is only applicable while there are a number of players greater than three.

Enacted, Proposal 319

Judgement

316. Judges
If players disagree about the legality of a move or the interpretation or application of a rule, then the player preceding the one moving is to be the Judge and decide the question. Disagreement for the purposes of this rule may be created by the insistence of any player. This process is called invoking Judgment.

When Judgment has been invoked, the next player may not begin his or her turn without the consent of a majority of the other players.

The Judge's Judgment may be overruled only by a 2/3 majority vote of the other players taken before the next turn is begun. If a Judge's Judgment is overruled, then the player preceding the Judge in the playing order becomes the new Judge for the question, and so on, except that no player is to be Judge during his or her own turn or during the turn of a team-mate.

Unless a Judge is overruled, one Judge settles all questions arising from the game until the next turn is begun, including questions as to his or her own legitimacy and jurisdiction as Judge.

New Judges are not bound by the decisions of old Judges. New Judges may, however, settle only those questions on which the players currently disagree and that affect the completion of the turn in which Judgment was invoked. All decisions by Judges shall be in accordance with all the rules then in effect; but when the rules are silent, inconsistent, or unclear on the point at issue, then the Judge shall consider game-custom and the spirit of the game before applying other standards.

Initial set, Rule 212,
Amended, Proposal 316

Decorum

306. Honorifics
Replaces Rule 305.

Each player possesses a variable set of honorific title, henceforth Honorifics, initially having none.

Honorifics may only be granted or renounced according to the text of rules.

A player may renounce any of their own Honorifics, unless doing so would violate a rule.

When a player refers to another by name in the channel #gameplay, they must include all of that player's Honorifics prior to the name itself. For each Honorific not included, the offending player must forfeit 10 points. The offending player subsequently obtains the Honorific "Wrong Dishonourable", which they must not renounce for at least three turns.

Each player possesses the Honorific 'Sir' if and only if it is currently their turn. This may not be renounced.

Each player possesses the Honorific 'Big Judge Man' if and only if they are currently Judge. This may not be renounced.

Enacted, Proposal 305,
Amended, Proposal 306

308. Victorious Honorifics
The winner of the previous game (henceforth winner) possesses a special type of Honorific for the length of the current game. They may not renounce this Honorific.

Whenever this player is referred to, an Honorific that has not previously been used to describe them during the course of the current game must be used, in addition to any Honorifics they would otherwise possess.

This Honorific must either refer to the fact that the player won the previous game or otherwise praise them generally.

Violating any of these rules shall carry the same punishment as neglecting to use a prescribed Honorific.

Enacted, Proposal 308.

Points & currencies

303. Score modification
No player may modify their score in any other way than as provided for by the ruleset.

Enacted, Proposal 303.

313. Millipedés

  1. Terminology
    1. Introduce a new legal term, by rule, meaning 'changeable only according to what is permitted explicitly or implicitly by the ruleset, not including Rule 116'.
    2. All quantities named of points and Millipedés in this rule are by rule.
  2. The Millipedé
    1. Introduce a currency called the Millipedé. Each player has an [sic] by rule amount of Millipedés, starting with zero. This amount is by rule non-negative. Each player may, at will, transfer at most as many Millipedés as they have to any other player.
    2. Millipedés have an exchange rate into points, initially set at 1.000 Millipedés per point. A player may claim at most 10 points worth of Millipedés per turn by giving up the equivalent amount of Millipedés. Points may not be exchanged into Millipedés, and the latter must be explicitly converted to value as the former.
  3. Universal Basic Income
    1. Each player is given 1.000 Millipedés per turn.
  4. OED lottery
    1. In the OED lottery, a player announces their guess for the length (number of letters) of the Oxford English Dictionary website's word of the day for the day after the guess was made.
    2. For the purposes of this section, days begin and end precisely when the word of the day changes.
    3. Every player who guesses correctly is each rewarded 10.000 Millipedés, plus a bonus of 2.500 Millipedés times the absolute difference between the word length and nine (9).

      For example, a word length of fourteen (14) would have a reward of 10.000 + 2.500 × |14 - 9| = 22.500, and a word length of six (6) would have a reward of 10.000 + 2.500 × |6 - 9| = 17.500.
    4. Each player may enter the OED lottery, at most once a day, at most once a turn. If a turn takes longer than a day, the player may only enter once during that day, and if there are multiple turns in a day, the player may only enter in a single turn in that day.
    5. If a player makes multiple guesses in the same day or turn, only the first guess is considered when determining points.

Enacted, Proposal 313.

Religion

317. Nomic Religion
To lighten the gentiles.

The Nomic Faith is hereby defined as the state religion of this game-state, Nomeland.

The head of the Nomic Faith, the Supreme Nome, bears the same honorific as his title for the length of his office.

The Nomic Court is a Nomic legal institution headed by the Supreme Nome. Its decisions are binding on all followers of the Nomic Faith, but not binding upon gentiles who belong not to the faith. A decision of the Nome Court, once promulgated, becomes a part of the Code of Nomic Law, which may not be modified any other way.

For the purposes of arbitration, the body of Nomic Law has the same authority as this civil law introducing the Nomic Faith.

Believers in the Nomic Faith may not take rule disputes between themselves to any institution but the Nomic Court, and must follow Nomic Law.

To convert to the Nomic Faith, believers must declare their trust in the Supreme Nome. Every player who begins their turn a believer is to be paid 2000 milipedés [sic] by the Supreme Nome. If the Supreme Nome cannot afford this, he may and must abdicate. A player may either join or leave the faith at most one time on their turn only, and must settle all outstanding disagreements before doing so.

The Supreme Nome may voluntarily relinquish his position to another believer at any time if he should so desire. Only one person may hold the title of Supreme Nome at a time. If the Supreme Nome's position is empty:

  1. The very first Supreme Nome shall be the first person to correctly answer a Confucian riddle proposed by Zenith after this law is passed. He obtains 10 000 milipedés as a reward for this correct answer.
  2. Otherwise, any player who can afford to pay the Nome faith payment for the current player may become the Supreme Nome.
  3. Otherwise all believers are excommunicated (this may be reversed by a future Supreme Nome) and the Supreme Nomeship enters abeyancy until legislation declares a new Supreme Nome.

Enacted, Proposal 317.

End of a game

208. Winning criteria
The winner is the first player to achieve 100 (positive) points.

In mail and computer games, the winner is the first player to achieve 200 (positive) points.

Initial set, Rule 208

213. Stalemate
If the rules are changed so that further play is impossible, or if the legality of a move cannot be determined with finality, or if by the Judge's best reasoning, not overruled, a move appears equally legal and illegal, then the first player unable to complete a turn is the winner.

This rule takes precedence over every other rule determining the winner.

Initial set, Rule 213

307. n-Tuple Nomic
When every player has had their turn once and the game goes into a round 2, it enters int a stage known as Double Nomic where any amount of points awarded or stripped is doubled from what it would be in the base game. When the game goes into round 3, the base points are instead tripled, bringing about Triple Nomic, and so on and so forth. This chain is reset and goes back to normal Nomic once a run ends.

A player may either join or leave the faith at most one time on their turn only, and must settle all outstanding disagreements before doing so.

The Supreme Nome may voluntarily relinquish his position to another believer at any time if he should so desire. Only one person may hold the title of Supreme Nome at a time. If the Supreme Nome's position is empty:

  1. The very first Supreme Nome shall be the first person to correctly answer a Confucian riddle proposed by Zenith after this law is passed. He obtains 10 000 milipedés [sic] as a reward for this correct answer.
  2. Otherwise, any player who can afford to pay the Nome faith payment for the current player may become the Supreme Nome.
  3. Otherwise all believers are excommunicated (this may be reversed by a future Supreme Nome) and the Supreme Nomeship enters abeyancy until legislation declares a new Supreme Nome.

Proposal 318
The Supreme Nome has the power to declare a state of emergency and decide how long this lasts. Under such a state, the Supreme Nome may repeal, amend, or create any law (rule) at any time (hour). This law (318) has as much precedence over other laws as is possible.

Proposal 319
In calculating the score for successful legislation, take the difference between the highest scoring player and the player who just passed legislation. Then divide by the number of players, and reward that quotient, rounded down to the nearest integer, as additional points.

For example, if the player with the highest score has 70 points, and I have 0, this rule would grant me (70 - 0) / 7 = 10 additional points. Further, this rule is only applicable while there are a number of players greater than three.

Proposed in game 4 on turn 3 by arolev, adopted 2025 Apr 24, following a 7/7 vote in favour.

Proposal 320
Nomic is closed every week for a time period from Saturday 01:00 to Sunday 09:00 (reckoned in UTC). No action taken during this time is considered part of the Nomic game. Outside of this time, game actions are as valid as in the rest of the rule set.
